OPTIONS FOR CAPTIONING

For people who are hard of hearing accessibility is paramount, and captioning serves as a crucial tool for ensuring inclusivity across various platforms.

Whether you're in need of real-time captioning, facilitated by Communication Access Real-Time Translation (CART), or seeking user-friendly captioning apps for your mobile or smart devices, we've got you covered.

Additionally, explore the convenience of captioning telephones, providing seamless communication experiences for individuals with hearing impairments.

Discover the diverse range of captioning solutions available and empower yourself to engage with content effortlessly.

CART

Communication Access Real-Time Translation (CART) is a specialised service designed to provide real-time text translation of spoken language into written text. CART is particularly beneficial for individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing, as well as those with auditory processing disorders.

Here's how it typically works: A trained CART provider, often using a stenotype machine or specialised software, listens to spoken dialogue in real-time, such as during meetings, lectures, conferences, or other live events. As they listen, they rapidly transcribe what is being said into written text, which is then displayed on a screen for the individual or audience to read. This instantaneous translation allows individuals to follow along with the conversation or presentation in real-time, providing them with access to information that they might otherwise miss.

CART can be delivered onsite, where the CART provider is physically present at the event, or remotely, where the provider listens to the audio feed and transcribes from a remote location. This flexibility makes CART a versatile and effective tool for ensuring communication access in various settings.

Overall, CART plays a vital role in promoting inclusivity and accessibility by breaking down communication barriers and providing real-time access to spoken language for individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing.

APPS

There are several captioning apps available that cater to the needs of individuals who are hard of hearing. These apps leverage speech recognition technology to provide real-time captioning for various audio and video content. Here are a few popular captioning apps:

Google Live Transcribe: Developed by Google, this app provides real-time transcription of speech to text. It captures spoken words and displays them as captions in near real-time. Users can also customise the text size and color for better readability.

Ava: Ava is a speech-to-text app that offers live captioning for conversations in real-time. It utilises artificial intelligence to transcribe speech from multiple speakers, making it ideal for group discussions, meetings, or social gatherings.

Otter.ai: Otter.ai is an AI-powered transcription app that generates accurate transcripts of audio recordings, including meetings, interviews, and lectures. It features real-time transcription, searchable archives, and the ability to differentiate between speakers.

Subtitle Viewer: Subtitle Viewer is an app that provides captioning for videos on mobile devices. It allows users to import videos from various sources and overlay captions in real-time, making it easier for individuals who are hard of hearing to follow along with the content.

CaptionMate: CaptionMate is designed specifically for individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing. It offers live captioning for phone calls, allowing users to read what the other person is saying in real-time during phone conversations.

These captioning apps vary in features and functionalities, but they all aim to provide accessible communication solutions for individuals who are hard of hearing, empowering them to engage with audio and video content more effectively in various situations.

CAPTIONED TELEPHONES

Captioned telephones are specialised phones designed to assist individuals who are hard of hearing or deaf by providing captions of the conversation in real-time. These phones work by displaying written captions of what the other party is saying during a phone call, allowing the user to read along while listening, which can greatly enhance comprehension and communication. Here are a few types of captioned telephones available:

CapTel Phones: CapTel (Captioned Telephone) phones are one of the most common types of captioned telephones. These phones feature a built-in screen that displays captions of the caller's words in real-time. Users can listen to the caller's voice and read the captions simultaneously, ensuring full access to the conversation.

Hamilton CapTel: Hamilton CapTel phones offer similar functionality to CapTel phones, providing real-time captions of phone conversations. They come with various models and features to suit different needs, including large displays, adjustable font sizes, and compatibility with hearing aids.

ClearCaptions Phones: ClearCaptions phones utilise a combination of voice recognition technology and live captioning services to provide accurate captions of phone calls. These phones are designed to be easy to use and offer features like speakerphone capabilities and customisable captions.

Ultratec Superprint: Ultratec Superprint phones are another option for individuals who are hard of hearing. These phones display captions of the conversation on a large built-in screen, making it easy for users to read along while talking on the phone.

CaptionCall Phones: CaptionCall phones combine amplified sound with captioning features to provide clear and accessible phone calls for individuals with hearing loss. These phones include features like adjustable volume, tone control, and customisable captions to meet the user's needs.

Overall, captioned telephones play a crucial role in facilitating communication for individuals who are hard of hearing or deaf, allowing them to stay connected with friends, family, and businesses through phone calls with confidence and ease.
