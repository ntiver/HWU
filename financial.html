FINANCIAL SUPPORT

Providing access to meetings or events is the event organiser's responsibility. As with any costs, communication access, like catering, should be built into the registration fees.  In this way, all participants share the costs of access.

Similarly, employers may be encouraging a barrier free and flexible workplace.  When information and communication systems are designed to be accessible, and to be interoperable with adaptive technologies, it is easier to accommodate employees’ requests for access to have equal employment opportunity and work effectively.

You may be eligible to source funding for the following captioning services:

Deaf Connect


Deaf Connect provides tailored services for deaf seniors to thrive in their communities. They offer communication assistance, social activities, information and referral services, advocacy, empowerment, and technology support. They aim to create an inclusive environment where deaf seniors can lead fulfilling lives, connect with peers, and access the support they need.

National Disability Insurance Service (NDIS)

Provides funding to eligible people with disability to gain more time with family and friends, greater independence, access to new skills, jobs, or volunteering in their community, and an improved quality of life.  Access to captioning may support your communication needs to pursue your goals.

Exmployment Assistance Fund (EAF)

A free Australian Government service administered by JobAccess available to help make modifications to the workplace as easily as possible.  People with disability and mental health condition who are already working, and about to start working, can apply for financial assistance to purchase a range of work-related modifications and services.  Reimbursement of captioning services can be requested as part of an EAF.
